ACADEMIC SUMMARY
Switch + Router Pairing & Hardening Phase

This document provides an academically oriented summary of the competencies,
security principles, and architectural decisions demonstrated during the
pairing and hardening process.

=====================================================================
1. Architectural Competencies Demonstrated
---------------------------------------------------------------------
- Layer-2/Layer-3 boundary design
- VLAN segmentation and trunking
- DHCP relay architecture
- ACL-based segmentation
- NAT boundary enforcement
- Control-plane protection (CoPP design and verification)
- Management-plane restriction
- Centralized logging integration

=====================================================================
2. Security Principles Applied
---------------------------------------------------------------------
- Zero-trust segmentation
- Principle of least privilege (VTY ACLs)
- Control-plane policing to protect router CPU
- DHCP Snooping trust anchoring
- STP hardening (portfast edge trunk, bpduguard)
- Service minimization (no HTTP, no CDP)

=====================================================================
3. Operational Rigor
---------------------------------------------------------------------
- Deterministic verification of each subsystem
- Validation of LLDP adjacency
- Validation of DHCP Snooping behavior
- Validation of CoPP class behavior
- Validation of logging posture
- Validation of management-plane restrictions
- Final state commit prior to documentation freeze

=====================================================================
4. Outcome
---------------------------------------------------------------------
The switch–router pairing is fully hardened, validated, and saved. This
documentation serves as the authoritative baseline for future audits,
rebuilds, and operational continuity.
